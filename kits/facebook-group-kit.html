01

Group name

Five options, mixing the brand name with beginner-magnet names a cold person instantly gets. A stranger scrolling Facebook should read the name and know "oh, that's for me."

1
Brand-led
Taylormade Academy

Clean, ownable, matches the brand and the academy. Downside: a cold beginner doesn't instantly know it's about AI — it reads as a name, not a benefit.

2
Brand + benefit
Taylormade Academy — Learn AI With No Code

Keeps the brand but adds the promise. "No code" is the magic phrase that tells nervous beginners they're allowed in. Searchable and self-explaining.

3
★ Recommended Brand + beginner magnet
Taylormade Academy — AI for Beginners (No Code, Free Tools)

The pick. It carries the brand AND does the cold-traffic job in one line. "AI for Beginners" is exactly what a behind-feeling person types and clicks. "No Code, Free Tools" removes the two biggest fears — being too technical and having to pay. A stranger gets it in half a second, which is what a top-of-funnel growth group needs.

4
Benefit-led
Build Real Things With AI (Beginner-Friendly)

Action-forward and warm. "Real things" sets us apart from prompt-trick groups. Slightly less brand equity, but a strong fallback if you want benefit over brand up top.

5
Beginner magnet
Beginner AI Builders (No Code · DFW & Online)

Names the exact person and the local angle, which helps early seeding from your DFW network. "Builders" creates identity — people want to call themselves that. Good for community feel, lighter on brand.

Why option 3 wins

For the front door of a funnel, clarity beats cleverness. Option 3 keeps Taylormade Academy in front (so the group, the ebooks, and the academy all feel like one world) while the parenthetical does the recruiting. You can always shorten the public display later once people know the brand — but for cold growth, lead with the words a beginner is already searching.

04

Group rules

Six rules that keep the room valuable and safe without killing the vibe. Paste into Facebook's rules section (each rule has a title + the description below it).

  1. Be kind — everyone here is a beginner at something. No talking down, no "you should already know this." We were all on day one once. Questions that feel basic to you might save someone else's whole week.
  2. No spam, no drops, no link dumps. Don't post your offer, affiliate link, or "DM me to make $5k" pitch. Posts like that get removed and repeat offenders get removed too. Share value, not promos.
  3. Do not DM members to pitch them. Sliding into people's inboxes to sell coaching, courses, or services is an instant removal. People came here to learn, not to get sold to in private.
  4. Keep it on-topic: building real things with AI. Agents, automations, simple apps, free tools, money ideas, wins, and questions all welcome. Off-topic, political, or unrelated content gets cleared out.
  5. Share what you build — wins big and small. Made your first automation? Built something that broke? Post it. Real attempts (even messy ones) teach more than perfect screenshots.
  6. No hate, harassment, or bigotry — full stop. Racism, sexism, homophobia, or any kind of harassment gets you removed with no warning. This is a welcoming room or it's nothing.
  7. Credit people, don't steal work. If you repost someone's idea, build, or prompt from in here, tag them. We grow by lifting each other.
05

Membership questions

Three questions new members answer when requesting to join. Q1 is the email capture with a real, friendly opt-in — phrased so people want to say yes. Turn on "require answers to join" in group settings.

★ Question 1 — Email capture + opt-in (most important)

What's the best email to send your free "Build Your First AI Agent" starter to? (Drop it below and you'll get it + a short weekly note from me with the best beginner builds. No spam, unsubscribe anytime.)

Why it works: people give the email because they're getting something real (the free starter), and the newsletter opt-in is stated plainly and honestly inside the same ask. Export these answers and add anyone who opts in to your list. Keep your promise — send the freebie and the note.

Question 2 — Engagement + segmentation

What's the one thing you want to build or automate with AI? (Even if you have no idea how — just tell me the dream.)

Why it works: it tells you what your audience actually wants (so you can make content for it), gets people emotionally invested before they even join, and gives you a warm reason to welcome them by name.

Question 3 — Connection + content fuel

What's your biggest question or fear about AI right now? (No question is too basic here — promise.)

Why it works: surfaces the exact objections and confusions to address in posts, makes nervous beginners feel safe, and hands you an endless supply of post and ebook topics straight from real people.

4-week content calendar

A sustainable rhythm for one person — five posts a week, same themes every week so it's easy to plan and members know what to expect. Below: the weekly skeleton, then four weeks of example prompts you can post almost as-is.

The weekly skeleton

A short, encouraging push for people who feel behind. One idea, one nudge to start.Ex: "You don't need to understand AI before you use it. You learn it by building one small thing. Here's your one small thing this week ↓"
TuesdayBuild-Along
The anchor post. One real thing to build this week, broken into beginner steps. Free tools only.Ex: "Let's build a tool that turns your messy notes into a clean to-do list. Step 1…"
ThursdayWin of the Week
Celebrate a member build, or post your own small win. Tag the person. Proof beginners can do this.Ex: "Look what [member] built after one week in here 👏 Tell us what you've made — wins big and small."
FridayAsk Me Anything
Open Q&A. You answer in the comments live-ish. Pulls out objections + gives you next week's topics.Ex: "Friday Q&A is open. Drop your AI question — beginner ones especially. I'll answer all day."
SundaySpotlight + Recap
Spotlight a member, recap the week, and point to the freebie/site. Soft funnel touch.Ex: "This week we built ___ and ___ shared a big win. New? Start free at academy.taylormadecreative.net ↓"

Solo-creator note

Five posts a week is enough — don't burn out chasing daily. Tuesday's build-along is the only one that takes real effort; the rest are short. Batch all five on one sitting and schedule them. Until you have member wins to feature, run Win of the Week and Spotlight on your own builds and the questions coming through your three membership questions. Never fake a member win.

How to launch it

The literal steps to stand the group up and get the first members in. Do these in order.

Set it up

  • Create the group. From your Facebook page or profile → menu (☰) → Groups → Create new group. Name it Taylormade Academy — AI for Beginners (No Code, Free Tools)
  • Set privacy to Public. For a top-of-funnel growth group, choose Public — not Private. Here's why: Public groups are discoverable in search and feeds, posts can show up to friends-of-members, and strangers can read before they join. That's exactly the reach a front-door funnel needs. (Private is better for paid/inner communities — that's what the on-site academy community is for. Keep this top layer open.)
  • Add the public blurb + full About description. Paste the tagline (Section 2) as the short description and the full description (Section 3) into the About section.
  • Upload brand visuals. Cover photo + group icon in Taylormade Academy colors (ink/paper/gold) with the tagline "No code. No hype. Just build the thing." Keep the text big and readable on mobile.

Turn on the funnel mechanics

  • Add the three membership questions. Group settings → Membership questions → add the three from Section 5. Toggle Require answers to request to join ON. This is your email-capture engine — don't skip it.
  • Set who can post / approval. Start with posts require admin approval ON for the first couple weeks to keep spammers out while the room is small. Loosen it once you have trusted members.
  • Add the rules. Settings → Rules → paste the six from Section 4 (title + description each).

Seed the content

  • Post the Welcome post and pin it. Publish Section 6, then click the post's ··· menu → Pin post.
  • Post the three starter threads. Publish the Intro thread, the Stuck thread, and the Quick-Win post (Section 7). Pin all three. (Facebook lets you feature several pinned/announced posts — use Announcements for these.)
  • Schedule week 1 of the calendar. Use Facebook's Schedule post option to load Monday–Sunday from Section 8 so the room never looks empty.

Seed the first members

  • Invite your warm circle first. Personally invite friends, family, and DFW network who'd genuinely be into this — even 10–20 real people make the room feel alive on day one. Don't mass-invite people who won't engage.
  • Announce it everywhere you already post. Drop the join link on your Instagram (@taylormade_creative), in your stories, on Threads, and in the academy newsletter. Lead with the free starter as the hook, not "join my group."
  • Link it from the academy. Add a "Join the free Facebook community" button on academy.taylormadecreative.net so site visitors funnel into the group, and group members funnel to the site. Two-way door.
  • Respond fast for the first two weeks. Welcome every new member by name, reply to every intro and question. Early responsiveness is what turns a quiet group into a sticky one.

The whole loop, in one sentence

Strangers find the Public group → they answer Q1 and join your email list → they get a real quick win and feel capable → you point them to academy.taylormadecreative.net for the free ebooks, courses, and deeper community. Front porch to front door. That's the move.
